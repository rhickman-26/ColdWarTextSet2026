        <h2>The Architecture of Containment (1945-1960)</h2>

    </div>

    <div class="stage-setter">

        <span class="stage-title">Setting the Stage: A World Divided</span>

        <p class="stage-text">World War II is over, but there is no peace. The United States and the Soviet Union—former allies—are now bitter enemies. America is afraid that the Soviets will spread communism across the globe. To stop this, U.S. leaders decide they must "contain" (stop) communism wherever it appears, leading to fear at home and new wars abroad.</p>

        <div class="vocab-row">

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Containment:</strong> The U.S. policy to stop the spread of communism.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Iron Curtain:</strong> The imaginary line dividing democratic West Europe from communist East Europe.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Red Scare:</strong> The intense fear that communists were hiding inside the U.S. government.</div>

        </div>

    </div>

    <div class="objectives-box">

        <div class="objectives-title">Mission Briefing: SSUSH20</div>

        <p class="learning-target">We are learning to analyze how U.S. foreign and domestic policies responded to the threat of communism during the Truman and Eisenhower administrations so that we can understand how early Cold War decisions shaped American global leadership and domestic culture.</p>

        <ul class="success-criteria">

            <li>I can explain how the Truman Doctrine and containment established America's role as the leader of the free world.</li>

            <li>I can describe how the Marshall Plan rebuilt Europe and expanded American influence.</li>

            <li>I can analyze how the Korean War demonstrated America's commitment to containing communism globally.</li>

            <li>I can identify how McCarthyism shaped American domestic culture and civil liberties.</li>

        </ul>

        <h2>Crises, Space, & Vietnam (1960-1968)</h2>

    </div>

    <div class="stage-setter">

        <span class="stage-title">Setting the Stage: On the Brink</span>

        <p class="stage-text">The Cold War gets dangerous. The U.S. and Soviet Union race to build more nuclear weapons and compete to see who can get to space first. Tensions are so high that one wrong move could destroy the world. Meanwhile, America sends thousands of soldiers into the jungles of Vietnam to fight communism, but the war becomes unpopular at home.</p>

        <div class="vocab-row">

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Space Race:</strong> The competition between the US and USSR to achieve spaceflight capability.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Domino Theory:</strong> The belief that if one nation fell to communism, its neighbors would follow.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Sputnik:</strong> The first artificial satellite, launched by the Soviets, which shocked America.</div>

        </div>

    </div>

    <div class="objectives-box">

        <div class="objectives-title">Mission Briefing: SSUSH21</div>

        <p class="learning-target">We are learning to evaluate how the Kennedy and Johnson administrations navigated Cold War crises, including the Space Race and the Vietnam War, so that we can understand how superpower competition influenced American foreign policy and military involvement.</p>

        <ul class="success-criteria">

            <li>I can explain the significance of the Space Race, Sputnik, and the moon landing.</li>

            <li>I can analyze the causes and consequences of the Cuban Missile Crisis.</li>

            <li>I can evaluate the causes of U.S. escalation in the Vietnam War.</li>

            <li>I can analyze the role of the media in shaping public opinion during the Vietnam War.</li>

        </ul>

    <div class="stage-setter">

        <span class="stage-title">Setting the Stage: The Long Road to Peace</span>

        <p class="stage-text">After decades of fear, things begin to change. President Nixon tries a new strategy called "Détente" to calm things down. He even visits communist China! But by the 1980s, President Reagan challenges the Soviet Union to tear down its walls. The Soviet economy crumbles, and the Cold War finally comes to an end.</p>

        <div class="vocab-row">

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Détente:</strong> A period of easing tensions and better relations between the US and USSR.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>Triangular Diplomacy:</strong> Nixon's strategy to pit communist China and the Soviet Union against each other.</div>

            <div class="vocab-item"><strong>War Powers Act:</strong> A law passed to stop the President from starting wars without Congress's permission.</div>

        </div>

    </div>
