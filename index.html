Gallery 1: The Architecture of Containment (1945-1960)

Setting the Stage: A World Divided

World War II is over, but there is no peace. The United States and the Soviet Union are now bitter enemies. America is afraid that the Soviets will spread communism. U.S. leaders decide to "contain" (stop) communism wherever it appears.

Containment: U.S. policy to stop the spread of communism.
Iron Curtain: Imaginary line dividing democratic West and communist East.
Red Scare: Fear of communists inside the U.S. government.
Mission Briefing: SSUSH20

Analyze how U.S. policies responded to the threat of communism during the Truman and Eisenhower administrations.

Harry S. Truman1945-1953
The Architect. Established "Containment" (Truman Doctrine), Marshall Plan, and committed US troops to Korea.
Dwight D. Eisenhower1953-1961
The General. Ended the Korean War; formulated "Domino Theory"; authorized U-2 spy planes.

Gallery 2: Crises, Space, & Vietnam (1960-1968)

Setting the Stage: On the Brink

The Cold War gets dangerous. The U.S. and Soviet Union race to build nuclear weapons and compete to get to space. Tensions are so high that one wrong move could destroy the world. Meanwhile, America sends thousands of soldiers into Vietnam.

Space Race: US vs USSR competition for spaceflight.
Domino Theory: Belief that if one nation falls to communism, neighbors will follow.
Sputnik: First artificial satellite (Soviet) that shocked America.
Mission Briefing: SSUSH21

Evaluate how Kennedy and Johnson navigated Cold War crises, the Space Race, and Vietnam.

John F. Kennedy1961-1963
Crisis Manager. Humiliated by Bay of Pigs; pledged Moon Shot; navigated Cuban Missile Crisis; Nuclear Test Ban Treaty.
Lyndon B. Johnson1963-1969
Wartime President. Escalated Vietnam War after Gulf of Tonkin; domestic "Great Society" overshadowed by war.

Gallery 3: Détente to Dissolution (1969-1991)

Setting the Stage: The Long Road to Peace

After decades of fear, things change. President Nixon tries "Détente" to calm things down and visits China. By the 1980s, President Reagan challenges the Soviet Union to tear down its walls. The Soviet economy crumbles, and the Cold War ends.

Détente: Easing of tensions between US and USSR.
Triangular Diplomacy: Nixon's strategy to pit China and USSR against each other.
War Powers Act: Law stopping President from starting wars without Congress.
Mission Briefing: SSUSH22-23

Analyze shift in policy from Vietnam to the collapse of the Soviet Union.

Richard Nixon1969-1974
The Strategist. Pursued "Détente"; opened relations with China; signed Paris Peace Accords ending US involvement in Vietnam.
Ronald Reagan1981-1989
The Challenger. Abandoned détente for "Peace through Strength"; increased military spending; challenged Gorbachev to "Tear down this wall."
